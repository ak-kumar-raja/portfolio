RFID-Based Personalized Reminder Display System for Assisted Living and Senior Care Facilities

A novel IoT system that uses RFID technology to automatically display personalized reminders (medication schedules, daily tasks, safety alerts) on digital screens when elderly residents are detected in specific areas of care facilities. The system improves care quality, reduces caregiver burden, and enhances resident independence through context-aware, location-triggered information delivery.

Innovation Overview

Problem: In assisted living and senior care facilities, elderly residents — particularly those with cognitive impairments such as dementia or Alzheimer's — often forget medication schedules, daily tasks, and safety protocols. Current reminder systems rely on manual caregiver intervention or static signage that doesn't personalize to individual needs.

Solution: An RFID-based system where each resident wears or carries an RFID tag. When they enter or approach designated areas (dining rooms, medication stations, activity centers, exits), RFID readers detect their presence and trigger personalized reminders on nearby digital display screens. The content is dynamically pulled from a centralized care management database that stores each resident's specific needs.

Key Technical Innovations:

  • Automatic resident identification via passive RFID tags (no battery, no charging)
  • Context-aware display content based on location zone + resident profile
  • Real-time integration with care management and medication scheduling systems
  • Multi-resident collision handling — priority-based display queuing
  • Caregiver notification system for missed medication or unusual patterns
  • Privacy-preserving design — no cameras, no audio surveillance

Market Application: The U.S. assisted living market serves 800,000+ residents across 28,000+ facilities. This patent addresses a growing need as the aging population increases and caregiver shortages intensify. The technology is applicable to hospitals, memory care units, rehabilitation centers, and home care settings.
